Food

Had a nice fish that had been caught from the very river passing through the city. Ate it while overlooking the river.

I decided to have ice cream while roaming the city, since it was fairly sunny that day. What could go wrong right? I had ordered a cone with my all-time favorite flavors: vanilla and lemon. Taste-wise they were alright, but there was another problem, the vanilla one looked yellow while the lemon one was snow-white! I guess they mixed-up the food colorings.

Places

Mostar city-center takes a person back to medieval europe, it is extremely well preserved, and very beatiful. Other than that, Blagaj Tekija is inside this municipality, as well as the town of Počitelj, which are both very well preserved historical sites.

  • Of course you should cross the historical bridge and everything like that, I won't go into detail for well-known things as such.
  • It is possible to get up to the minaret of Koski Mehmed Pasha mosque, which is a must-do if you are not afraid of height. It has the best view of the historical city. The mosque itself is also nice.
  • In the backstreets, there are coppersmiths who you can hear or watch working. Souvenirs can be bought from them which would also support this long existing profession of the city.
  • Blagaj Tekija is a "tekke" which is a center for Islamic mysticism. In addition to being a historical place with a great nature around it, it is still used as a tekke, and they hold "zikirs" after the night prayer three times a week. Luckily, the day we were visiting was a zikir day so we got to experience it. Zikir is an act of worship where people gather together to recite the names of God.
  • Počitelj is home to an Ottoman castle, which is abandoned today and it is possible to freely roam inside the castle, which I like better than going into castles as a "tourist" with tickets and supervision. All the building in this small town are historical, making the atmosphere fantastic. The population of the city is very low during winter, but its inhabitants move back in for summer.

People

  • In mostar, met with a coppersmith, who claimed that the copperware in Mostar is much better than in Sarajevo.
  • In Blagaj, met with a very strange South African fella who has been living in Bosnia for the past three months, in his caravan. It was a very long chat, but the moral of the story was that, South African whites are subjected to racism both in South Africa and in the United Kingdom, so he was in search of other places to live, he said. He had a very suspicious way of income, trading certain goods with locals.
  • In Blagaj Tekija, thanks to the lady who informed us about the Zikir that was gonna take place that night, we were able to attend it. All of the attendees (6-7 people other than us, because it was an off-season) were also very friendly, and they gave us food, "burek", while they were just having snacks.
